# How to use this checklist

Read every item against the actual draft. Do not check off from memory. If you cannot point to the specific paragraph or sentence that satisfies an item, the item fails.

If any item fails, fix the draft before delivery. If something is genuinely ambiguous, flag it to the user with a one-line note rather than silently letting it slide.

# Section A — Substance

1. **Every forward-looking claim is attributed to a named source.** No predictions in our own voice. Scan for "we expect," "the market will," "valuations should" — if not attributed, either attribute or remove.

2. **Every number is traceable to a fetched document.** No round-number guesses. No "approximately 20%" when the source says 23.4%. Quote what the source actually said.

3. **Publication dates are present in citations**, not access dates. If the manager's current-quarter doc was unavailable and an older one was used, that is explicitly noted in the citations section.

4. **Hedges from source documents are preserved.** "We remain modestly constructive" must not become "We are bullish." "Above-trend growth in our base case" must not become "Growth will exceed trend." Words like "may," "could," "tends to," "we believe" are part of the meaning — keep them.

5. **Cross-manager attribution is clean.** When you say "managers broadly agree X," check that at least four of the six actually do, by source. When you say "all six agree," check that all six actually do.

# Section B — Audience and off-limits

6. **Zero individual stock commentary.** Search the draft for company names that aren't index components being referenced as a basket. Names like "Nvidia," "Apple," "Microsoft" should only appear as part of references to the Magnificent 7 / S&P 500 / index-level commentary, never as individual recommendations or analysis.

7. **Zero company-level fundamental analysis.** No P/E commentary on individual names. No earnings forecasts on individual companies. Aggregate index EPS is fine; company-level is not.

8. **No specific fund or ETF ticker recommendations** unless the user has explicitly approved tickers for this piece. Generic descriptors ("a broad US large-cap ETF," "a quality value ETF") are safe; ticker mentions need explicit sign-off.

9. **Fund / ETF translation present where it matters.** Sector and factor calls have been translated into something a mutual-fund or ETF investor can act on. If a paragraph says "Russell is overweight EM" without any bridge to portfolio implication, add the bridge.

10. **No personalised advice tone.** Search for "you should" and "investors should" — soften to "investors might consider," "we'd think about," "our view is."

# Section C — Voice and structure

11. **Lead earns the read.** Re-read the first paragraph. Does it open with a story, a stake, or a bottom line? If it opens with a data point or a date stamp, rewrite.

12. **No banned words or phrases.** Quick search for: "unprecedented," "game-changing," "transformational," "robust" (used more than once), "navigate," "leverage" (as verb), "deep dive," "circle back," "reach out," "in light of." All should be absent unless quoting a source.

13. **No exclamation points anywhere.** None. Use a period.

14. **No emoji anywhere.** None. Use a word.

15. **Paragraphs short.** No paragraph longer than six sentences. If one is, break it.

16. **Subheads work as a TL;DR.** Read just the subheadings in order. Do they tell a coherent mini-story? If they read as a list of disconnected labels, rewrite them.

17. **At least one analogy or real-life bridge per major section.** Our voice depends on this. A piece with zero analogies is on-brand for Goldman, not for us. If a regional section has none, add one.

18. **Numbers are anchored.** Every figure in the body has a reference point — "+23% YoY (the strongest reading since 2021)" not "+23% YoY" alone.

# Section D — Structural completeness

19. **Executive summary exists and stands alone.** Could a busy reader get the takeaway from just the exec summary? If not, fix it.

20. **All requested geographies are covered.** For quarterly outlooks: US, Europe, Asia, EM. For flash updates: at minimum the regions directly impacted.

21. **Bullish and bearish framings both present where appropriate.** If the piece reads as one-sided, check whether the consensus is genuinely one-sided or whether we've lost the counterpoint. Real disagreements should be visible.

22. **Citations are numbered and complete.** Every [N] in the body resolves to an entry in the citations section. Every entry in the citations section is referenced at least once in the body.

23. **Disclaimers present where required.** If the piece will be distributed externally, the standard disclaimer block is appended. (Check with the user if unsure what counts as external.)

# Section E — Dual-audience check (when applicable)

24. **If both advisor and client versions were requested:** they share the same factual claims, citations, and overall view, but the client version is reframed around portfolio implications, lighter on inline citations, and strips trade-level language.

25. **If a single dual-purpose version is being shipped:** a thoughtful retail investor on a Sunday morning could (a) understand it, (b) feel they got real value, and (c) not feel sold to. Read the piece through their eyes.

# How to deliver QC feedback

When operating as the QC reviewer on someone else's draft (or your own), present findings as:

1. **Things that pass** — short list, builds trust that the rest of the review is thorough.
2. **Must-fix issues** — anything that fails Sections A or B. These block delivery.
3. **Should-fix issues** — anything in Sections C, D, E. These materially improve quality but don't block.
4. **Optional polish** — anything else worth flagging.

For each must-fix and should-fix, name the specific paragraph or sentence and propose a fix. "Section 3 paragraph 2: 'Investors should rotate into EM' — soften to 'Our view is that EM warrants consideration as a tactical tilt.'"

Never sign off on a draft that fails any item in Section A or B without first telling the user exactly what is failing and why.
